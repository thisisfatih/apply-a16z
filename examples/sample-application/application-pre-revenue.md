## Company name
Mirro

## One-line description
Mirro is the first mental health platform built specifically for first-generation immigrants — therapist matching by cultural background, language, and immigration experience.

[▸ WHY THIS WORKS]
No revenue to put here (so no ARR embed possible). Instead: extreme specificity of user ("first-generation immigrants"), specific differentiator ("cultural background, language, immigration experience"). Passes the "could this be said about 50 other companies?" test — no. The word "first" is a risk (usually suspicious), but defensible: no existing platform does three-way matching on cultural background + language + immigration experience.

## What does your company do?
68% of immigrants who need mental health support don't seek it — not because of stigma, but because they can't find a therapist who understands what it means to send remittances home while managing a visa, or to navigate grief for a country you can't return to. Mirro matches first-generation immigrants to therapists who share their immigration background and primary language. Matching takes 3 minutes. First session within 48 hours. We've run 47 intake sessions with beta users. 41 of 47 said the match felt "immediately different" from prior therapy experiences.

[▸ WHY THIS WORKS]
Opens with specific insight ("not because of stigma, but because...") — this is the earned secret. Mechanism specific (3 minutes, 48 hours). Pre-revenue so no $ metric — uses behavioral validation instead (41/47). Specific failure mode named (can't find culturally-competent therapist). Passes Emily Bennett's test: "who is the user, what is the problem, what have you built?"

---

## What is the problem?
There are 45M first-generation immigrants in the US. 68% who need mental health support don't seek it. Existing platforms (BetterHelp, Talkspace) match on insurance and availability — not on the specific cultural pressures that drive immigrant mental health crises: deportation anxiety, remittance stress, intergenerational role reversal, cultural grief. I spent 8 years as an immigrant mental health advocate, ran support groups in three states, and watched hundreds of people cycle through mismatched therapists and give up. The dropout rate in my groups after "tried therapy, didn't help": 73%. The reason: the therapist didn't understand the context.

[▸ WHY THIS WORKS]
The founder IS the user. "8 years as an immigrant mental health advocate" + "ran support groups in three states" = maximum earned access. Fareed's test: "what's the thing you can teach me that I wouldn't have otherwise seen?" Answer: the 73% dropout rate from mismatched therapists in community support groups. That's a data point nobody else has. That's the earned secret.

---

## What is your solution?
Mirro's matching algorithm uses three signals competitors ignore: (1) therapist's own immigration background (which country, how long ago, documented or undocumented experience); (2) primary language AND dialect; (3) familiarity with specific immigration pathways (work visa, asylum, DACA, family reunification). Therapists self-select into our platform because they're underutilized elsewhere — their specialization doesn't appear on BetterHelp's filters. We charge $20/session to users (below market, driving volume) and take 20% of therapist revenue. No insurance billing initially — removes the barrier for undocumented users.

[▸ WHY THIS WORKS]
Three specific matching signals named. Distribution insight embedded (therapists are underutilized elsewhere = pull, not push acquisition). Business model clear with the unit ($20 × 20%). Explicit design choice for undocumented users = demonstrates problem depth. Pre-revenue but the model is credible.

---

## Business model
$20/session, 20% take rate = $4 net per session. Target: 10 sessions/user/year = $40 LTV per user. With 1,000 MAU in year 1: $40K ARR. Therapist side: we charge no subscription — they earn more than BetterHelp rates ($65-80/hr vs. their typical $40-55/hr on other platforms) because our users are high-engagement. Path to unit economics: therapy bundles (12-session packages at $200) + group sessions for higher margin.

[▸ WHY THIS WORKS]
Shows math. Bottom-up. Not "the market is $X billion." Explains why therapist supply is sustainable (they earn more). Weakness here: LTV of $40 per user is thin — the eval would flag this. The application should note the bundle path more prominently. Score: 3/5.

---

## Why now?
Two things changed: (1) Telehealth normalization post-COVID removed the location barrier — a Guatemalan immigrant in rural Iowa can now see a therapist from their home country; (2) the 2023 CAA created a new pathway for paroled immigrants, adding 350K+ new arrivals who are specifically excluded from Medicaid for 5 years. This population is underserved, English-limited, and uninsured. No existing platform is building for them. The policy window is 3-5 years before Medicaid eligibility opens up.

[▸ WHY THIS WORKS]
Two specific enabling factors. Named policy (CAA 2023) with specific consequence (350K arrivals, 5-year Medicaid exclusion). This is the Kenan Saleh test: "people who really know the history of the market" — this founder knows a policy change that most mental health founders don't know exists. The "3-5 year window" claim is specific and falsifiable.

## Who are your competitors and how are you different?
BetterHelp (40M users) and Talkspace match on insurance and availability — they have no immigration-aware matching signals. Alma and Headway target the therapist supply side but don't solve matching for immigrant users. Culturally-specific platforms exist for Black mental health (Therapy for Black Girls) and LGBTQ+ (Violet) — they proved the model works for underserved identity groups. Mirro is the same pattern applied to immigrants. Our moat: the therapist community trusts us because we were built by an advocate they already know. BetterHelp can't replicate that trust without years of community presence.

[▸ WHY THIS WORKS]
Named all competitors honestly. Learned from adjacent category (Therapy for Black Girls) — shows market research depth. Structural moat named: community trust from 8 years of advocacy work. Josh Lu: "maniacal about understanding competition." This is that.

## Why are you the right team?
Ana Reyes (CEO): 8 years running immigrant mental health support groups across CA, NY, TX — personally facilitated 1,200+ sessions, trained 40 community facilitators, built the network of culturally-competent therapists Mirro is now onboarding. Marcus Wu (CTO): built therapist-matching infrastructure at Headway for 3 years, owns the engineering playbook for HIPAA-compliant session management. We have the community trust that makes therapist acquisition free, and the engineering depth to not rebuild what Headway already solved.

[▸ WHY THIS WORKS]
"Personally facilitated 1,200+ sessions, trained 40 community facilitators" — this is the Dinner Party Jerk test. Embarrassingly specific. "Built the network of therapists we're now onboarding" = distribution advantage that a funded competitor can't buy. Marcus's background specific ("HIPAA-compliant session management" not "engineering experience"). Last sentence is the team thesis: community trust + no need to rebuild infrastructure.

## What do you want from a16z beyond capital?
Introductions to community health organizations in the a16z network for partnership conversations — specifically organizations that work with newly-arrived immigrants. Guidance on the Medicaid billing pathway we'll need to build in year 2. Would value connections to founders who've navigated the healthcare compliance stack (HIPAA, telehealth regulations).

## How are you using AI?
Matching algorithm uses LLM-powered therapist profile parsing — we extract cultural signals from therapist bios that standard filter systems miss ("I moved from Colombia at 22" in a bio paragraph → tagged as South American immigration experience, adult arrival, 3+ years). GPT-4o for intake session summarization to match on presenting issues without requiring users to repeat their story to each potential therapist. Not using AI for therapy itself.
