# Application Field Guide

Complete field list for the a16z speedrun application form. Includes field name, character/word limit, what the field is actually asking, and common mistakes.

> **Research note:** The form is email-gated at speedrun.a16z.com/apply. No founder has published the exact verbatim wording. Fields below are confirmed from official a16z partner guidance, FAQ, and accepted founder accounts. Character limits are inferred from "no essays" guidance and 3–5 sentence recommendations — treat as soft limits.

**Application writing process endorsed by a16z team:**
1. Write raw — all thoughts, unfiltered
2. Run through AI to compress and clarify
3. Rewrite in your own voice
4. Verify: sounds like a strategy memo, not a pitch?

---

## Core Fields

### 1. Company name
**Limit:** Short text
**What they want:** Name only. No tagline.

### 2. One-line description
**Limit:** ~100 characters
**What they want:** Who you help, what you do, measurable outcome. No adjectives.
**Pattern:** "[Company] helps [who] do [what] so they can [outcome]."
**SR006 pattern:** 32% of accepted companies put ARR directly in the one-liner.
**Examples (from funded companies):**
- "Bilrost automates commercial loan processing — $680K ARR in 5 weeks."
- "Concorda is the AI OS for trial lawyers — $210K ARR in 3 months."
**Mistake:** Starting with "We are building a platform..."

### 3. What does your company do?
**Limit:** 150-200 words
**What they want:** The Velcro answer — something that sticks in 10 seconds. Problem + solution + why it works now.
**Mistake:** Describing the market before describing what you do.

### 4. What is the problem you are solving?
**Limit:** 150-200 words
**What they want:** Evidence the founder has lived this problem or deeply interviewed people who have. Specific pain, not category pain.
**Mistake:** "Companies struggle with X" — no specificity, no evidence of depth.

### 5. What is your solution?
**Limit:** 150-200 words
**What they want:** What you built, how it works, what makes it different from the obvious alternatives.
**Mistake:** Feature list. Lead with the mechanism and the outcome.

### 6. What is your business model?
**Limit:** 100-150 words
**What they want:** How do you make money? Per seat, usage, percentage of spend, annual contract? What's the unit?
**Mistake:** "We plan to monetize through multiple revenue streams." Name the primary stream and the unit economics.

### 7. Who are your competitors and how are you different?
**Limit:** 150 words
**What they want:** Shows you know the landscape. Honest differentiation — not "no one else does this."
**Mistake:** Listing only obvious losers as competition, or claiming no competition.

### 8. Why now?
**Limit:** 100-150 words
**What they want:** Specific enabling factor that makes this possible today but not 3 years ago.
**Examples:** Model cost/capability threshold, new API availability, regulatory change, distribution unlock.
**Mistake:** "AI is transforming every industry." Not an answer.

### 9. What is your traction?
**Limit:** 150 words
**What they want:** Numbers with denominators. Growth rates. Named customers if possible.
**Format:** "X customers, $Y ARR, Z% month-over-month growth, N weeks since launch."
**Mistake:** "Strong early interest" / "positive feedback from users."

### 10. Why are you the right team to build this?
**Limit:** 200 words
**What they want:** Biographical answer. What have you built, shipped, sold, or broken that makes you uniquely qualified?
**Mistake:** Listing credentials (degrees, employers) instead of outputs (products built, customers signed, code shipped).

### 11. What do you want from a16z beyond capital?
**Limit:** 100 words
**What they want:** Specific asks — intros to enterprise customers, go-to-market expertise, a particular partner's domain knowledge.
**Mistake:** "Mentorship and guidance." Generic.

### 12. How are you using AI?
**Limit:** 150 words
**What they want:** Concrete description of AI's role in the product. Not "we use AI to improve efficiency."
**Mistake:** Treating this as optional. It is not — this is a speedrun program.

## Supplemental Fields (may vary by cohort)

### 13. Where are you based?
**Limit:** Short text

### 14. How did you hear about speedrun?
**Limit:** Short text

### 15. Links
Product demo, GitHub, website, deck. A working demo URL outweighs all other signals.

**Critical:** Deck link must be publicly accessible. Private links = reviewers skip it.
Deck: 7–15 slides, large font. Josh Lu prefers 7. Max 15. Structure: Problem → User → Solution → What's built → Traction → Market → Team → Vision.

**Avoid:** Advisor slides, Harvey Ball competitive matrices (feature checkbox tables), dense text slides.

### 16. Founders
Names, roles, LinkedIn or GitHub. One sentence per founder on their relevant prior output.

## Field completion checklist

Before submitting each field, verify:
- [ ] Contains at least one specific number, name, or date
- [ ] No forbidden phrases (see `docs/a16z-speedrun-criteria.md`)
- [ ] Answers the question asked, not an easier version of it
- [ ] Could not have been written by someone who hasn't talked to customers
